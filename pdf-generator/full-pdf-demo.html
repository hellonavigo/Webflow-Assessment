Welcome to The Attributes Assessment

Instructions

‍

The first step to becoming an Optimal Performer is self-discovery—recognizing our attributes. Attributes are innate traits that we all have. We are high on some, low on others, and average on most of them. Understanding which ones we're high and low on, helps us understand ourselves better and gives us food for thought on how to develop and improve ourselves.

‍

The list of 42 attributes is divided into 9 categories. You can take all 9 of these assessments below, or you can pick the ones you're most interested in. Once you've taken an assessment, the results of the Attributes in that category will show in the Results section on this page. The more assessments you take, the more Attributes you'll see appear in the list.

‍

Every assessment presents between 35 and 55 questions, and will take you around 6 minutes to complete.

‍

The nine categories

Attributes can be divided into two main parts: there are the attributes that are important for self-optimization, and those important for group optimization.

‍

Categories that are important for Self-Optimization and their Attributes:

‍

  • Grit: Courage, Perseverance, Adaptability, Resilience
  • Drive: Self-Efficacy, Narcissism, Discipline, Competitiveness/Non-Competitiveness
  • Mental Acuity: Situational Awareness, Compartmentalization, Task Switching, Learnability, Discernment
  • Commitment: Confidence, Persistence, Tenacity, Optimism, Patience/Impatience
  • Vision: Creativity, Innovativeness, Open-Mindedness, Curiosity, Cunning

‍

‍

Categories that are important for Group-Optimization and their Attributes:

‍

  • Social Intelligence: Emotional Intelligence, Charisma, Influence, Introversion/Extroversion, Humor
  • Service: Compassion, Empathy, Generosity, Selflessness, Caring
  • Teamability: Integrity, Conscientiousness, Humility, Collaborative, Insouciance/Fear of Rejection
  • Leadership: Authenticity, Decisiveness, Accountability

‍

‍

How to answer the questions

When selecting your response options to each statement it may help to picture yourself in specific real-life scenarios (either at home or at work). Environments and situations where attributes are most visible tend to be subjective for each individual, so recalling those that apply to you will help you answer more authentically. Answer openly and honestly by indicating how you actually are - not how you would like to be or how you would prefer to be seen. Remember, there are no “right” or “wrong” answers and no “good” or “bad” choices.

‍

After you have completed all the items, please be sure to submit your responses.

About Results

The scores are numbers between 1 and 7. This score indicates if you are low, medium or high on this Attribute. Note that it is normal to score medium on most of your Attributes. You’ll want to pay attention to your 5 highest Attributes (your strengths) and your 5 lowest Attributes (room for improvement).

The development of the Attributes assessment is grounded in cognitive, behavioral, and emotional sciences. The goal of the assessments is to identify and provide insight into the levels of the fixed and dormant attributes you possess. This assessment is about helping you with optimal performance, by highlighting your tendencies and pinpointing both your strengths and development opportunities.
